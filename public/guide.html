Kansas Driver's Test — Study Guide

A complete, plain-English summary of the entire Kansas Driving Handbook (AAMVA / Feb 2022), condensed for studying. Practice tests and the answer key are separate files.

The knowledge test: 25 multiple-choice questions on traffic laws, signs, and pavement markings.
To pass: 80% — 20 of 25 correct. Not timed (~15–20 min). Can be taken at a license office or online at ks.knowtodrive.com.
Retakes: 4 attempts allowed; after the 4th fail you must wait 6 months ($1.50 re-test fee).
Vision: Must be at least 20/40 in one eye (glasses/contacts OK).

1. LICENSE, PERMITS & THE LAW

  • - A Kansas license is required to drive on public roads; you must get one within 90 days of moving to Kansas.
  • - Driving is a privilege, not a right — it can be suspended or revoked.
  • - Class C license = passenger vehicles under 26,001 lbs.

Graduated Driver's Licensing (GDL) — teen stages:

  • - Instruction Permit (14+): Drive only with a licensed adult 21+ (with 1+ years' experience) in the front seat. No wireless device use. No other person but the adult in the front seat.
  • - Restricted License (15): Held learner's permit 1 year; limited driving (to/from school, work, religious activities); no non-sibling minor passengers; supervised by a licensed adult.
  • - Less-restricted (16): May drive 5 a.m.–9 p.m. and to school/work/religious activities; only one non-sibling minor passenger.
  • - Unrestricted (17): All age-related restrictions removed (after holding restricted license 6 months past age 16).

Losing your license:

  • - 3 moving violations within 12 months → suspension.
  • - Refusing a chemical (BAC) test → 1-year suspension (1st refusal; longer for repeats).
  • - DUI, no insurance, failure to appear, leaving an accident → suspension/revocation.

Documents to bring (general): proof of identity/lawful presence (List A/B), Social Security proof, and two proofs of Kansas residency. Real ID (for flights/federal buildings after May 3, 2023) requires originals (not laminated/photocopied).

Organ donor: Anyone 18+ can register as a donor on their license (first-person consent — legally binding). Under 18 needs a parent's signature.

Specific signs worth memorizing

  • - No Turn on Red → may NOT turn on red; wait for green.
  • - Stop Here on Red → stop at this line on red (right turn still allowed after stopping).
  • - Do Not Enter (white bar in red circle) → wrong way / don't enter.
  • - Wrong Way → you're going the wrong direction; turn around.
  • - Keep Right / Slower Traffic Keep Right → stay right unless passing.
  • - Shared Center Lane (Left Turn Only) → middle lane for left turns from both directions only — never through traffic or passing.
  • - Slow-Moving Vehicle (orange/red triangle) → vehicle going under 25 mph (farm equipment, buggies).
  • - Route markers: shield shape/color shows interstate, U.S., or state route.
  • - Exit signs: "Exit" on the left of the sign = exit is on the left; on the right = exit on the right.

5. RIGHT-OF-WAY (who goes first)

Key idea: the law only says who must YIELD. It never gives you the right-of-way — you must make sure it's safe.
  • - Pedestrians always win — yield even if they jaywalk.
  • - Blind pedestrian (white cane or guide dog) → always stop. Don't honk (it scares them).
  • - Turning left → yield to oncoming traffic going straight or turning right.
  • - Uncontrolled intersection (no signs) → yield to the car on your right.
  • - 4-way stop → first to arrive goes. Tie? The car on the right goes.
  • - Entering from a driveway/alley/private road → yield to traffic already on the road.
  • - Roundabout/traffic circle → yield to traffic already in the circle; go counterclockwise; slow to 15–20 mph; don't change lanes or pass; signal right before your exit.
  • - Emergency vehicle (lights/siren) → pull to the right and stop; stay 500 ft back.
  • - Never enter an intersection (even on green) unless you can get all the way through.

6. STOPPING & SCHOOL BUSES

Come to a FULL STOP for:

  • - A stop sign, a red light, or a flashing red light
  • - A school crossing guard holding a flag
  • - A flag person at a railroad crossing or construction site
  • - An emergency vehicle approaching with lights/siren
  • - Pulling out of an alley/driveway in a business or residential area

School buses:

  • - Stop when a school/church/daycare bus has red lights flashing or its stop arm out — whether it's on your side OR the oncoming side of an undivided road (two yellow lines between).
  • - Exception: On a divided highway, traffic going the opposite direction does not stop.
  • - Stay stopped until the red lights stop flashing and the stop arm folds in.

7. SPEED, STOPPING & FOLLOWING DISTANCE

  • - Basic Speed Law: Never drive faster than is reasonable and prudent for conditions — even below the posted limit (rain, fog, traffic). 55 mph in a snowstorm is not legal just because it's posted.
  • - 2-second rule (good conditions); add a second for rain, night, gravel, heavy loads, tailgaters, or following motorcycles/large trucks.
  • - It's illegal to drive so slowly you block traffic; driving 15+ mph slower than traffic flow is as dangerous as speeding.

Stopping distance (a frequent test topic):

  • - Total stopping distance = perception + reaction + braking distance.
  • - Perception time for an alert driver ≈ ¾ to 1 second.
  • - Doubling speed (20→40 mph) makes braking distance 4× longer; tripling (20→60) makes it 9×; 80 mph ≈ 16× the impact of 20 mph.
  • - At 60 mph it takes about 4–5 seconds / ~360 feet to perceive and stop.

Merging / changing lanes: Look for a 4–6 second gap. Check mirrors and your blind spot (over your shoulder) before moving. Use the acceleration lane to match traffic speed; don't stop at its end. Don't cross a solid white line.

8. PASSING

  • - Pass on the left. Signal, make sure it's clear, don't exceed the speed limit.
  • - Return only when you see the passed car in your rearview mirror — and before you're within 200 feet of any oncoming car.
  • - Do NOT pass when:
  • - Within 100 feet of a bridge, tunnel, intersection, or railroad crossing
  • - On a hill or curve, or any marked no-passing zone (you can't see 1/3 mile / ~20–25 sec ahead)
  • - There's a solid yellow line on your side
  • - Within 100 feet of a stopped emergency vehicle (lights on)
  • - Passing on the right is allowed only on one-way streets or multilane roads going the same direction. Never pass on the shoulder.
  • - If you're being passed: keep right, hold a steady speed, don't speed up.

9. ALCOHOL, DRUGS, DISTRACTION & FITNESS TO DRIVE

  • - Illegal at .08 BAC or higher (adults). Under 21 = zero tolerance. Vision is affected at just .02 BAC.
  • - Alcohol/drugs are in about 40% of fatal crashes.
  • - Only time sobers you up — not coffee, food, or a shower. Body removes ~one drink per hour.
  • - Implied Consent: Having a license means you've agreed to a breath/blood/urine test if an officer asks. Refusing = lose your license 1 year.
  • - Prescription and over-the-counter drugs can impair you like alcohol — check labels.
  • - Texting/writing/reading on a device while driving is illegal in Kansas.
  • - Fatigue: If tired, pull over for a 15–20 minute nap — don't "push through."
  • - Cell phone: best practice is to stop in a safe spot to call/text.
  • - Emotions: Angry or upset? Cool off before driving. Aggressive driver? Get out of their way, don't make eye contact, don't challenge them.

10. SEAT BELTS, AIR BAGS & CHILD SAFETY

  • - Seat belts required for ALL occupants — a primary violation (police can stop you just for that).
  • - Wear belts even with air bags — air bags don't protect in side impacts or rollovers and won't keep you behind the wheel.
  • - Shoulder belt across the shoulder/chest (never under the arm or behind the back); lap belt low and snug across the hips.
  • - Under 4 → child safety seat. 4–8 → safety/booster seat unless taller than 4'9" OR over 80 lbs → then a seat belt. 8+ → seat belt.
  • - Children 12 and under should ride in the back seat.
  • - Never put a rear-facing infant seat in front of an active airbag. Keep ~10 inches between your chest and the steering wheel.

11. PARKING

No parking:

  • - Within an intersection or crosswalk
  • - Within 15 feet of a fire hydrant
  • - More than 12 inches from the curb
  • - In front of a driveway, on a sidewalk, on a bridge, or in a tunnel
  • - Within 30 feet of a stop sign, signal, or flashing beacon
  • - Within 50 feet of a railroad crossing
  • - Within 20 feet of a fire-station driveway (75 ft on the opposite side)
  • - In a handicapped space without a permit

Parking on a hill (test favorite):

  • - Downhill → turn wheels toward the curb.
  • - Uphill WITH a curb → turn wheels away from the curb.
  • - No curb (either way) → turn wheels toward the shoulder/edge.

(Logic: if your brakes fail, the curb or roadside stops the car instead of rolling into traffic.)

12. SHARING THE ROAD

  • - Bicyclists have the same rights/rules as cars; they ride on the right and use hand signals. Give them room when passing; don't honk close to them. Watch for "Dead Red Law" (a cyclist may cross on a light that won't detect them).
  • - Motorcycles/mopeds get a full lane. They're hard to see — check blind spots. Follow at 3–4 seconds. Don't assume they're turning just because a signal is on (signals may not self-cancel). A moped has ≤ 3.5 hp and tops out at ~30 mph.
  • - Emergency vehicles (siren/lights): pull to the right and stop. Stay 500 feet back.
  • - Move-Over Law: Approaching a stopped emergency/tow vehicle with flashing lights → move over a lane if you can, or slow down.
  • - Large trucks/buses have huge blind spots ("No-Zones") — front, rear, and both sides. If you can't see the driver's mirror, they can't see you. They swing wide for turns — never squeeze between a turning truck and the curb. A loaded truck at 55 mph needs 1½× a car's stopping distance.
  • - Transit bus: You do not have to stop for a transit bus (unlike a school bus) — but watch for pedestrians around it.
  • - Slow-moving vehicles (farm equipment, animal-drawn): orange/red triangle = under 25 mph. Pass with caution; they may swing left before a right turn.
  • - Trains: Never shift gears on the tracks. If you stall on the tracks, get out and run toward the direction the train is coming from (debris flies away from you). A 100-car train at 55 mph needs over a mile to stop. Always expect a train.

13. BEFORE YOU DRIVE & BASIC CONTROL

  • - Adjust seat and mirrors before driving. Top of the steering wheel no higher than your shoulders; ~10 inches from your chest to the wheel.
  • - Head restraint should contact the back of your head (not below your ears) to prevent whiplash.
  • - Steering — preferred method is hand-to-hand (push/pull), hands at 9 and 3 (or 8 and 4). The old 10-and-2 is not recommended because of airbags. Use one hand only when backing or operating controls.
  • - Backing: Check behind the vehicle first, go slow, turn and look over your shoulder through the rear window (mirrors don't show directly behind you). It's illegal to back in a travel lane except to parallel park.
  • - Before starting: make sure the parking brake is set, then start. Before stopping: check mirrors for traffic.
  • - Traction technology: ABS lets you brake and steer at the same time without skidding — press firmly and hold (don't pump). Remember Search, Stomp, Steer. ESP (stability control) helps prevent skids.

14. VISUAL SEARCH & COMMUNICATING

  • - Scan well ahead — look 12–15 seconds down the road (city: ~2 blocks; highway: ~¼ mile). Keep your eyes moving; check mirrors every few seconds and before any lane change, slow-down, or hill.
  • - Headlights: Turn them on when it gets dark, in rain/snow/fog, and whenever your wipers are on (required in Kansas). High beams see twice as far — but dim them within ~one block (500 ft) of an oncoming car or when following someone.
  • - Horn: A light tap to warn someone who may not see you. A sharp blast only in real danger. Don't use it to greet people, hurry others, or near blind pedestrians/horses.
  • - Signaling: Signal 100 ft before a turn in town (200 ft above 40 mph), or 3–4 seconds ahead. Cancel it after the turn.
  • - Disabled vehicle: Get off the road, turn on hazard flashers, raise the hood or tie a white cloth, set flares 200–300 feet behind.

You must also obey a school crossing guard at all times while they direct traffic.

15. NIGHT, WEATHER & EMERGENCIES

Night:

  • - Use high beams when no oncoming traffic; dim within ~one block. Don't stare into oncoming headlights. Never wear sunglasses at night.
  • - Low beams light ~150–200 ft (safe to ~45 mph); high beams ~350–400 ft (safe to ~65 mph).

Weather & traction:

  • - Roads are most slippery when rain/snow/sleet FIRST starts (water mixes with oil and dirt).
  • - Reduce speed and increase following distance on gravel/dirt, wet, or icy roads. Bridges and overpasses freeze first.
  • - Hydroplaning starts around 35 mph on wet roads (worse with worn tires) — slow down in the rain.
  • - Adverse weather: if you can't see more than 100 feet, don't go faster than 30 mph; pull over if it's too bad.
  • - Studded snow tires are legal Nov 1 – Apr 1 only.

Skids / losing traction: Look where you want to go, ease off the pedals, and steer toward your intended path — don't slam the brakes or oversteer.

Emergencies:

  • - Brake failure: pump the pedal, then use the parking brake gradually, then steer to a safe stop.
  • - Tire blowout: grip the wheel, don't brake hard, ease off the gas, let it slow, pull off.
  • - Stuck accelerator / power loss: shift to neutral, steer to the side, brake gently.
  • - Avoiding a crash: you can usually steer away faster than you can stop. With ABS, brake hard and steer.
  • - Rollovers: mostly single-vehicle; caused by speed and oversteering. SUVs/vans/pickups are more prone. Seat belts are the best protection.
  • - Flooded road: turn around. 6 inches of water can move a car; 2 feet can sweep it away.
  • - Animals: don't swerve — brake and stay in your lane. Deer are most active at dawn/dusk and Oct–Dec.

Seasonal (optional section):

  • - Winter: carry a survival kit; if stranded, stay with the vehicle, run the engine ~10 min/hour, keep the exhaust pipe clear, crack a window.
  • - Hot weather: watch tire pressure, coolant, and "bleeding tar" (slippery).
  • - Tornado: leave the vehicle for sturdy shelter; don't hide under an overpass.
  • - Lightning: stay inside a hard-topped vehicle with windows up; don't touch metal.
  • - Trip planning: limit driving to ~6–8 hours/day (one driver); avoid driving after 11 p.m.

✅ Final Tips for Test Day

  • - Take the knowledge test online at ks.knowtodrive.com or at a license office.
  • - Read each question fully — wrong answers are often "almost right."
  • - When unsure, pick the safest, most cautious option — it's usually correct.
  • - Re-read the 1-Page Cheat Sheet right before you go in.
  • - Then run the practice tests (separate files) until you're scoring 90%+.
